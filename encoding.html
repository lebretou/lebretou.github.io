Positions along a common scale

In encoding type "positions along a common scale", numerical values are represented by the location of points along a common bounded axis or interval. In our example, you will see two points within a common rectangular box. We are using the edges of the rectangle to represent an XY coordinate system. The horizontal (X) axis runs along the bottom edge of the box, while the vertical axis runs along the left edge of the box. Your task is to compare the vertical (y) positions of these two points, ignoring their horizontal (x) positions. Please focus on determining which point is higher or lower in the box. Positions along identical, non-aligned scales

In encoding type "Positions along identical, non-aligned scales", numerical values are represented by the locations of points along identical bounded axes or intervals. In our example, you will see two points within two identical but non-aligned rectangular boxes. We are using the edges of the rectangles to represent an XY coordinate system. The horizontal (X) axes runs along the bottom edges of the boxes, while the vertical axes runs along the left edges of the boxes. Your task is to compare the relative vertical (y) positions of these two points within its respective box, ignoring their horizontal (x) positions. Textures

In encoding type "textures", numerical values are represented by density of a cross-hatching pattern. In our example, numerical values are represented by how closely spaced the lines in the pattern are to each other. In other words, the more crowded a pattern appear to be the higher the density.
