Study Instructions

Welcome! Thank you again for participating in our study. On this page, we present instructions on how to complete the trials. Please carefully read the following instructions as your performance is dependent on understanding the task.

Instructions

Our study involves three parts: “visual”, “tactile”, and “tabular”.

  • The “visual” part of the study will involve you inspecting some simple geometric shapes on a computer display. This condition has 42 trials. You will first identify the smaller visual encoding. Then, in a number between 0 and 100, you will estimate what the proportion of the smaller encoding is to the larger one.
  • The “tactile” part will involve inspecting some geometric shapes on embossed paper by touching the paper with your hands and answering questions. You will be blindfolded with a piece of cloth for this part of the study. This condition has 42 trials. You will first locate the tactile pair on the embossed paper. Then, same to the "visual" condition, you will answer orally to the study administrator which one is the smaller encoding. Finally, you will answer to the study administrator a number between 0 and 100 of what the proportion of the smaller encoding is to the larger one.
  • Finally, the “tabular” part will involve viewing a data table on a computer display or using a screen reader and answering questions.  This condition has 7 trials. In this condition, you will only need to answer to the study administrator a number between 0 and 100 of what the proportion of the smaller encoding is to the larger one.

In a single trial across all three conditions, you will compare a pair of values encoded in visual, tactile, tabular form. Upon comparing the patterns, you will orally answer to the researcher:

  • which one (left or right) in the pair is of the smaller quantity.
  • Then, you will answer again and provide a number between 0 and 100 to indicate the proportion of the smaller element (from your previous answer) to the larger one.
Note that since both “visual” and “tabular” parts will be conducted using a computer, you will complete these two parts together on a computer in random order. You will perform the “tactile” part of our study separately on paper while blind-folded. For trials that were completed using both computer and embossed paper, we organized them into blocks of 7 trials. In visual conditions, each trial will be displayed on a separate webpage. After every 7 trials, you will encounter a "page break," which allows you to choose whether to continue or not. Similarly, in tactile conditions, we will pause after every 7 trials.

Encoding Types

In our study, we encoded numerical value pairs using various visual/tactile elements. The below sections will explain how values are encoded and represented in these encodings. Note that "visual" and "tactile" present same types of encodings on different mediums. It is important for you to understand the relationship between numerical value and encoding types.

Lines

In encoding type “lines”, numerical values are represented in lengths. You will inspect the pair of lines directly by comparing their difference in lengths. An example of a pair of two lines encodings:

Areas

In encoding type "areas", numerical values are represented in areas in circles. The area of a circle is the measure of the amount of space enclosed by the circle. You will inspect the pair of circles and compare their difference in areas. An example of a pair of two area encodings:

In this example, "Area B" is smaller in its enclosed region than "Area A", thus it is the smaller element in the pair. "Area B" is 50% of "Area A" in area.

Curves

In encoding type "curves", numerical values are represented in the value of curvature. Curvature is a measure of how much a curve deviates from being a stright line.
